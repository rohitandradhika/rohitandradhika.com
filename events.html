Thursday, July 10, 2014 | Time: To be announced

Mehndi

Radhika's House

905-812-2726

Address: To be announced

All the girls from Radhika and Rohit's side are asked to come together on this day to get their henna done while bonding with the bride. Henna (also called Mehndi) is the dye used to decorate the hands and feet of the bride, and as well as the hands of her friends. The bride also gets her future husbands initials henna tattooed on her hands, but they are hidden for the groom to find! After this function, the bride is not allowed to leave her house till her wedding. You are welcome to arrive anytime till 6:00PM, since after that begins the Choora ceremony.

Thursday, July 10, 2014 | Time: To be announced

Choora

Radhika's House

905-812-2726

Address: To be announced

This evening, Radhika's close family is invited to come watch this important function where her maternal uncle and aunt (Mamu and Mami) will present Radhika with her bridal bangles (choora), which will then be blessed by the priest and put on the bride to then wear for her wedding! The choora signifies the newlywed status and is worn for good omen. After this, the bride's sisters will tie the kalire to the bangles, which are hanging ornaments worn for good luck.

Friday, July 11, 2014 | Time: To be announced

Sehra Bandi

Rohit's House

905-495-1475

Address: To be announced

The morning of the wedding, Rohit's sisters are invited to tie the headpiece (sehra) on the groom. This is a short ceremony to complete his attire for the wedding before he leaves for the ceremony site. The sehra has garlands hanging that cover the groom's face, and is worn to protect the groom from "the evil eye". Once this is done, head over to the ceremony site and join the baraat with lots of dancing to start off the wedding!
